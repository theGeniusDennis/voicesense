

# **HCI Lab – University of Ghana: Project Context Writeup**

## **1. Overview of the HCI Lab**

The **Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Lab** at the **University of Ghana** is a research-focused lab dedicated to studying how humans interact with computers and digital systems. The lab’s primary goal is to **design, evaluate, and improve technology interfaces** to ensure they are **user-friendly, efficient, and inclusive**.

### **Key Research Focus Areas**

* **Voice and Language Interfaces**: Designing systems that understand spoken and written human language.
* **Human-Centered AI**: Studying AI systems with the human user in mind, including usability and accessibility.
* **Assistive Technologies**: Creating solutions that help people with disabilities or low-tech literacy.
* **Machine Translation & Multilingual Systems**: Focusing on **low-resource languages**, particularly African languages.
* **User Experience (UX) Studies**: Measuring how humans interact with software systems and optimizing for ease-of-use.

The lab is well-known for combining **theoretical research with practical applications**, producing publications, datasets, and prototypes that have both academic and societal impact.

---

## **2. Track Chosen: Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)**

### **2.1 What ASR Is**

**Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)** is the technology that allows computers to **convert spoken language into text**. It is a critical component in modern **voice assistants, speech-to-text tools, and interactive voice-based applications**.

In the HCI Lab context, ASR research focuses on:

* **Understanding diverse speech patterns**, accents, and dialects.
* Creating **accurate recognition systems for low-resource languages**, including African languages.
* Optimizing the **human experience**, e.g., reducing errors in recognition and improving response times.
* Integrating ASR with **Text-to-Speech (TTS) and Machine Translation (MT)** systems to build complete human-computer interaction pipelines.

---

### **2.2 Importance of ASR in the Lab**

* **Human-Centered AI**: The lab studies **how people interact with voice systems**, identifying challenges like misinterpretation or accessibility issues.
* **Research Potential**: ASR in African languages is still an **emerging research field**, offering opportunities for publications and innovative solutions.
* **Integration with HCI Studies**: ASR systems provide the **first layer of interaction**, feeding into user experience studies, NLP processing, and downstream AI tasks like intent recognition or translation.

## **3. Relevance to Your Project – “VoiceSense”**

Your chosen project, **VoiceSense**, directly aligns with the HCI Lab’s ASR research track:

### **How it aligns**

1. **NLP + ASR Focus**:

   * VoiceSense simulates **speech-to-text processing** by classifying text inputs into **intents**, mirroring the **first step of ASR pipelines**.

2. **Human-Centered Design**:

   * The system is designed to **interpret user commands accurately**, which is a core HCI concern: usability, efficiency, and reliability.

3. **African Language Relevance** (Future Extension):

   * While initially in English, VoiceSense can be extended to **African languages**, supporting the lab’s research on **low-resource multilingual ASR systems**.

4. **Interactive Interface**:

   * Using **Streamlit**, the project provides a **real-time web interface**, allowing researchers and users to **test and evaluate system behavior**.

5. **Research Foundation**:

   * The project demonstrates:

     * **Text preprocessing**
     * **Intent classification**
     * **Interactive testing**
   * These are **foundational skills for any ASR research project**, making it highly suitable for academic work in the lab.
